Today, the dominant culture of Venezuela reflects a blend of indigenous and Spanish customs, as well as regional Caribbean and Andean influences. Venezuelans are often described as warm, welcoming and open people.

El Carnival Festival in Venezuela is celebrated in the months of February and March based on the traditional calendar. The date can always change since the liturgical calendar is ruled by the lunar cycle, and it occurs at the same time as the beginning of the Christian Lent.

Carnival in Venezuela is derived from Christianity. During ancient times, the celebration focused on indulgence and carnal satisfaction. With time, the emphasis of the celebration changed, but the rhythms of music and the colorful costumes persisted.

During colonial times, the carnival consisted of physical games where men and women danced in the streets showing what was described as immoral gestures. In the 18th century, the Bishop of Caracas, Diez Madroñero, transformed the celebration into three days of prayers, rosaries and parades.

As years went by, Mayor José Abalos, gave the carnival its original meaning and emphasized purified components. Those who would witness the parades would receive candies, floats, and be encouraged to take part in it as a way to remove the violence that was prevalent in the past.

The carnival of the International Fair of the Sun or Carnaval Taurino de América is a celebration that takes place every year during the carnival season in the city of Mérida, Mérida state. It is a particular carnival, as it does not function as a traditional carnival, but rather functions as a fair that manages the different agricultural sectors of the state. In addition to this, some bullfights are held within the facilities.

Carnivals are festivals where sports, creativity, culture, folklore, music and religion are celebrated. People gather to witness the various corridas de toro and corridas de toro and the carnival parades. Likewise, a Reina de Carnaval or Reina del Sol is also crowned, which is selected a few days before the fair through a beauty contest.

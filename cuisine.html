Venezuelan cuisine is influenced by its European West African, and indigenous traditions. Venezuelan cuisine varies greatly from one region to another.
   
Arepa Andina is a variety of Venezuelan arepas, distinguished from the regular arepas by the usage of wheat flour rather than the standard corn flour. This variety of arepa is popular in the Andes region, near the Colombian border, especially in the state of Mérida, where wheat has been cultivated since ancient times. of Mérida,where wheat has been cultivated since ancient times.

 

Cachapas are giant pancakes made from fresh corn that are topped with soft cheese and various meats. They are one of  Venezuela's most popular dishes. They are sold everywhere there, from luxury restaurants to modest food trucks.

Cachitos are a Venezuelan food similar to the croissant, and are often filled with ham and cheese. The ingredients vary according to the state and the bakery they are made from but the most common ingredients are: Wheat flou, butter, eggs, room temperature milk, water, salt, yeast and pinch of sugar.
